Why are the coral reefs in decline?

Anthropogenic Actions

      Human activities are the primary cause of coral reef destruction. These activities include pollution, overfishing, and coastal development. These threats lead to a decline in biodiversity, affecting the balance of marine ecosystems crucial for the health of our planet.

Lack of Awareness

      Coral reef awareness is a critical issue in the Philippines due to widespread reef degradation caused by destructive fishing practices, pollution, and climate change, which threaten marine biodiversity and coastal communities. Limited public knowledge and enforcement of conservation efforts further hinder effective protection and restoration of these vital ecosystems.

Landscape Alteration

      Land alteration in the Philippines, such as deforestation and coastal development, leads to increased sediment runoff into marine environments, which smothers coral reefs, blocks sunlight essential for photosynthesis, and disrupts reef ecosystems. Additionally, the removal of mangroves and seagrasses for aquaculture and urban expansion exacerbates sedimentation and pollution, further degrading coral health and biodiversity.
